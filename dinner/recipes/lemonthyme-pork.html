Lemon Thyme Pork

with Roasted Zucchini and Garlic Herb Rice

Serves: 2

Prep Time

15 min

Cook Time

20 min

Total Time

35 min

Ingredients

  • 1 Zucchini
  • 1 Lemon
  • ¾ cup Rice
  • 12 ounce Pork Cutlets
  • 2 tablespoon Garlic Herb Butter
  • 2 tablespoon Sour Cream
  • Thyme
  • Chicken Stock
  • Cooking Oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Utensils

  • Rice Cooker (or Small Pot)
  • Large Pan
  • Baking Sheet
  • Paper Towel

Preparation

  1. Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to 450 F°. Wash and dry all produce. Trim and halve zucchini lengthwise; cut crosswise into ½-inch-thick half-moons. Quarter lemon.
  2. Add rice and 1 cup water to rice cooker and cook. If a rice cooker isn't available in a small pot, combine rice, 1 cup water, and a big pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce to a low simmer. Cook until rice is tender, 15-18 minutes. Keep covered off heat until ready to serve.
  3. While rice cooks, toss zucchini on a baking sheet with a drizzle of oil, salt, and pepper. Roast on top rack, flipping halfway through, until browned and tender, 14-16 minutes. Remove from oven and cover to keep warm.
  4. Meanwhile, pat pork dry with paper towels; season all over with salt and pepper. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add pork and cook until browned and cooked through, 2-3 minutes per side. Turn off heat; transfer to a plate. Wipe out pan.
  5. Melt half the garlic herb butter (youâ€™ll use the rest later) in same pan over medium-high heat. Add chopped thyme and cook until fragrant, 30 seconds. Stir in a splash of stock and ¼ cup water; bring to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes, then remove pan from heat. Stir in sour cream, 1 TBSP plain butter, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Season with pepper.
  6. Fluff rice with a fork; stir in remaining garlic herb butter. Taste and season with salt and pepper. Divide rice, pork, and zucchini between plates. Spoon sauce over pork. Serve with remaining lemon wedges on the side.
