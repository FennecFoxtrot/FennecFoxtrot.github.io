Magnifico Mozz Melt

with Balsamic Shallots and Bell Pepper Relish, Basil Aioli and Crispy Potatoes

Serves: 2

Prep Time

5 min

Cook Time

35 min

Total Time

40 min

Ingredients

  • 1 Bell Pepper
  • 12 ounce Gold Potatoes
  • 1 Shallot
  • 1 teaspoon Garlic Powder
  • 5 teaspoon Balsamic Vinegar
  • 4 tablespoon Mayonnaise
  • 4 slices Sourdough Bread
  • 1 cup Mozzarella Cheese
  • Dried Basil
  • Cooking Oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Utensils

  • Aluminum foil
  • Baking sheet
  • Small bowl
  • Large pan

Preparation

  1. Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to 425 F°. Wash and dry all produce. Halve bell pepper lengthwise; remove stem and seeds. Cut potatoes into ½-inch-thick wedges. Halve, peel, and slice shallot. Toss shallot on a piece of foil with a drizzle of olive oil, half the vinegar, salt, and pepper; cinch into a packet.
  2. Toss potatoes on one side of a baking sheet with a drizzle of olive oil, half the garlic powder (you’ll use more later), salt, and pepper. Place bell pepper and foil packet with shallot on opposite side of same sheet. Drizzle bell pepper with olive oil and season with salt and pepper; rub to coat. Arrange cut sides down. Roast on top rack until bell pepper and shallot are browned and tender, 15-20 minutes (you’ll roast potatoes a bit longer).
  3. Once bell pepper and shallot are done, transfer to a cutting board. Give potatoes a toss. Return to top rack until browned and crispy, 5-10 minutes more. Turn off oven; leave potatoes inside to keep warm until ready to serve.
  4. In a small bowl, combine mayonnaise, basil, and a big pinch of remaining garlic powder to taste. Season with salt and pepper
  5. Finely chop bell pepper and balsamic shallot together to form a relish. Season with salt and pepper. Spread half the sourdough slices with relish; sprinkle with mozzarella. Spread remaining slices with half the basil aioli. Top mozzarella with remaining slices, aioli-sides down.
  6. Heat a large drizzle of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add melts and cook until bread is golden brown and cheese has melted, 4-6 minutes per side. TIP: If necessary, add another drizzle of olive oil after flipping sandwiches. Halve melts on a diagonal. Divide between plates and serve with potatoes and remaining aioli on the side.
