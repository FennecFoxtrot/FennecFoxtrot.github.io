Chicken Pineapple Quesadillas

with Pico de Gallo and Southwestern Spices

Serves: 2

Prep Time

10 min

Cook Time

20 min

Total Time

30 min

Ingredients

  • 1 Red Onion
  • 2 Green Bell Peppers
  • 8 ounce Pineapple
  • 12 ounce Chicken Breast
  • 2 Roma Tomatos
  • 1 Lime
  • 2 tablespoon Southwest Spice Blend
  • 4 Flour Tortillas
  • 2 cup Mozzarella Cheese
  • 4 tablespoon Sour Cream
  • Cooking Oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Utensils

  • Small Bowl
  • Large Pan
  • Strainer
  • Baking Sheet

Preparation

  1. Wash and dry all produce. Preheat broiler to high or oven to 500 F°. Halve, peel, and thinly slice onion. Mince a few slices until you have 2 TBSP minced onion, then set this aside in a small bowl. Core, seed, and thinly slice bell peppers. Drain pineapple. Cut chicken into ½-inch cubes.
  2. Heat a large drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add sliced onion and bell peppers. Cook, tossing occasionally, until softened, 4-5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Add pineapple and cook, tossing, until lightly browned, 2-3 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Remove from pan and set aside.
  3. Meanwhile, finely chop tomatoes. Halve lime. Add tomatoes and a squeeze of lime to bowl with minced onion. Toss to combine. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Add a large drizzle of oil, chicken, and Southwest spice to pan used for veggies over medium-high heat. Cook, tossing, until chicken is no longer pink in center, 3-4 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Return veggie mixture to pan along with a squeeze of lime. Toss to combine, then remove pan from heat.
  5. Rub one side of each tortilla with a drizzle of oil. Place oiled-side down on a baking sheet. Take half the mozzarella and sprinkle it on tortillas in an even layer. Top with filling, then sprinkle with remaining mozzarella. Fold tortillas in half to create four quesadillas.
  6. Broil or bake quesadillas in oven until golden brown on top, 1-2 minutes. Flip and repeat with other side. TIP: Keep an eye on quesadillas to make sure they donâ€™t burn. Cut into wedges, then serve with pico de gallo and sour cream on the side.
