Cheesy Loaded Potato Rounds

Serves: 2

Prep Time

5 min

Cook Time

25 min

Total Time

30 min

Ingredients

  • 12 ounce Gold Potatoes
  • 1 tablespoon Fry Seasoning
  • 2 ounces Bacon
  • ½ cup Cheddar Cheese
  • 2 Scallions
  • Cooking Oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Utensils

  • Baking sheet
  • Large pan
  • Paper towel

Preparation

  1. Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to 450 F°. Wash and dry all produce. Cut potatoes into ¼-inch-thick rounds. Toss on a baking sheet with a large drizzle of oil, half the Fry Seasoning (you’ll use the rest later), salt, and pepper. Arrange in a single overlapping layer. Roast on top rack until lightly browned, 20 minutes (you’ll add more to the sheet then).
  2. Heat a large pan over medium-high heat. Add bacon and cook, turning occasionally and adjusting heat if browning too quickly, until crispy, 6-10 minutes. Turn off heat; transfer bacon to a paper-towel-lined plate. Carefully discard all bacon fat. Once bacon is cool enough to handle, finely chop the slices.
  3. Once potatoes have roasted 20 minutes, remove from oven and evenly sprinkle with cheese and chopped bacon. Return to oven until cheese melts and potatoes are tender, 5-7 minutes more. Trim and thinly slice scallions.
  4. Garnish potatoes with sour cream and scallions and serve.
